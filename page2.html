"Ranger" Electrified 4X4

4-motor Torque Vectoring

Independent wheel control enables dynamic manipulation of vehicle properties, including promotion and mitigation of oversteer. Torque at the wheel is modulated with respect to more than 8 different vehicle state metrics, like directional accelerations and drivemode.

Validated hybrid drivetrain

Custom "hybrid-electric" driveline uses bang-bang style control to optimize energy usage, resulting in a validated 18% reduction in fuel consumption. This is true in steady and unsteady state conditions, following typical driving patterns.

Real time state estimation

Using inertial information, and vector-based vehicle properties, Ranger's stack calculates dynamic state in real-time. This includes critical information for the torque vectoring system, such as slip angle.

Ranger's RTOS stack was written in Lua, designed to operate on a 60hz cycle.

MV-22B "Osprey"

Avionics: reverse-engineered

Thousands of lines of code, modeled after NATOPS publications enable simulation of dozens of electrical, hydraulic, and mechanical avionics systems. Control outputs are calculated in real-time with more than a dozen variables to emulate real flight stability characteristics.

Representative MFDs

Multi-Function Display pages are modeled after their real counterparts, with 15+ possible pages per display. All four displays can control aircraft systems, with 10+ components relying on MFDs for state control.

1:1 to the real thing

Cross-over between my replica and the real aircraft is incredibly high. The real NATOPS flight manual can be used to start my replica, and vice-versa. A real pilot was interviewed during development, for model validation.

45,000+ users

Continued demand, even years later - over 4,000 word technical documentation supports the model, with upkeep for critical features. 

Designed to real documentation
4 independent displays, with video overlays
Critical autopilot modes are replicated
Representative fuel system supports AAR
