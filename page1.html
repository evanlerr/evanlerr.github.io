Data Acquisition

Path

I was a member of Sun Devil motorsports for a huge majority of my undergraduate studies. I started as a mechanical engineer, lightly supporting shock position sensors, to eventually growing to lead the Data Acquisition sub-team. 

Technical Flexibility

Managing an electromechanical system means jumping between disciplines. I learned to utilize tools like KICAD and RapidHarness to support electrical system development, and the STM32/platformIO development environments for embedded systems.

"Wheel Boxes"

During my time in SDM, I took ownership of the wheel box assemblies, that colocated sensitive electronics in the wheel area onto the outboard, primarily measuring wheel-speed and brake thermal data. These went through two iterations, with the second version incorporating a new "Variable Reluctance" sensor for wheel speed, aimed directly at the machined wheel bearing spacer. 

SDM-25 Steering Wheel

One of my favorite projects was having the ability to help take the new digitized steering wheel module from blank slate to integration and execution. I did the PCB layout, then wrote the firmware for the module, and integrated it into the vehicle.
